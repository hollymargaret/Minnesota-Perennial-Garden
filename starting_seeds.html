Starting Seeds Indoors

Starting your own seeds can be an inexpensive way to grow a perennial garden. Starting seeds yourself also provides a sense of pride, wonder and acomplishment when they come to full bloom in your garden.

Follow these steps to start seeds indoors.

  1. Make sure seeds are fresh. Check the date on the seed envelope.
  2. Refer to the "days until germination" to find out how early to start the seeds before they will begin to sprout from seed shell. Use this information to determine when to start your seeds.
  3. Find a warm, sunny spot to place seed cells pots, or use special growing lights.
  4. Use plastic "cell packs", or egg cartons with steril potting soil filled to the top.
  5. Sow seeds four times the height of your seed deep into the potting soil.
  6. Water seeds just enough for the potting soil to become moist. Do not overwater.
  7. Check soil regularly to make sure soil stays moist. Rewater as much as necessary.
  8. Transplant seedlings when the plant is hearty enough to withstand the outdoor elements. Some seedlings may need to be aclimated to their new habitat which you can do by setting them outside for partial days and bringing them in at night until they seem hearty enough to overnight.
